Thesis

Train an agent with Supervised Learning using Unity3D

The project features the development of an application, using Unity, in which the user applies the supervised machine learning technique to train an agent. The application is a 2D puzzle game in which the player designs a labyrinth and drives a vehicle through the labyrinth in order to collect training data. After that, an agent is trained to drive autonomously based on players driving behavior. When the training is finished, the agent tries to escape the labyrinth.

About Me

I am a graduate from "School of Electrical and Computer Engineering" at Aristotle University of Thessaloniki. In 2018, I participated in a game development workshop for making games with Unity at University. The combination of math, art and programming in game development fascinated me. After that experience, I was sure of what I want to do with my life.
On games that I made myself, I did most of the programming by following tutorials, online courses and by using programming techniques/patterns that I learned from University. Also, in my spare time, I did some of the art assets and sounds for this games.
Most of my projects are made as member of a team called "2 Weeks Games". We are a 3-person team that loves to create games. My role in the team is lead programmer. I am responsible for programming the core game mechanics and complex new behaviours that the game designer of the team came up with.
